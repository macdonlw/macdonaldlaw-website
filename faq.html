Frequently Asked Questions

I've been charged with a criminal offence. What should I do first?

Contact a lawyer immediately — before speaking to anyone else about the charges. You have the right to remain silent and the right to counsel. Early legal advice is critical to protecting your rights and building a strong defence. Call MacDonald Law Office at (613) 283-0012 for an urgent consultation.

Do I need a lawyer for a bail hearing?

Yes. A bail hearing determines whether you will be released from custody while your case proceeds. Having a lawyer who can present a strong release plan to the court significantly improves your chances of being released on reasonable conditions. MacDonald Law Office is available 24/7 for bail hearings.

Will I have a criminal record if I'm charged?

Being charged is not the same as being convicted. A criminal record results only from a conviction or a finding of guilt. There are many possible outcomes, including withdrawal of charges, a peace bond, a discharge, or an acquittal at trial. Mark P. MacDonald will advise you on the realistic options in your case.

How much does it cost to hire a criminal defence lawyer?

Legal fees depend on the complexity of the charges and how the case proceeds. MacDonald Law Office offers transparent fee discussions at the outset so you know what to expect. Legal aid may be available depending on your financial circumstances and the seriousness of the charges.

How long does a real estate closing take?

A typical residential real estate closing in Ontario takes 30 to 90 days from the date the agreement of purchase and sale is signed. MacDonald Law Office can work within tighter timelines when required.

What happens if I die without a will?

If you die without a valid will in Ontario, the Succession Law Reform Act determines how your estate is distributed — and it may not match your wishes. Your spouse and children have statutory entitlements, but common-law partners, stepchildren, and close friends may receive nothing. A properly drafted will ensures your assets go where you want them to go.

Do I need both a will and powers of attorney?

Yes. A will takes effect only after death. Powers of attorney for property and personal care take effect during your lifetime if you become unable to manage your own affairs due to illness or incapacity. Together, these documents form a complete estate plan.
